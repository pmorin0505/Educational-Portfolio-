# CyberTrust Massachusetts CTF – APT43 Defensive Range Exercise

## Overview

This project documents my participation in the CyberTrust Massachusetts Cyber Range Exercise hosted through Immersive Labs and organized by Meghan Martinez. The simulation focused on a defensive cybersecurity scenario involving APT43 (Kimsuky), a known advanced persistent threat group.

The exercise replicated a real-world attack chain in a controlled environment, allowing participants to analyze logs, detect malicious activity, and understand attacker behavior from initial access to full domain compromise.

---

## Key Experience

As a freshman, this was my first cybersecurity capture-the-flag (CTF) event of this scale. I initially felt overwhelmed, but it quickly became an extremely valuable learning experience.

Due to scheduling issues, I completed the exercise independently. While I originally formed a team, I ended up handling the CTF, analysis, and presentation on my own. This reflected a real-world cybersecurity environment where professionals often work under time pressure and must rely on their own skills.

I completed:
- 4-hour timed CTF environment
- Full technical analysis of a multi-stage cyber attack
- Executive briefing presentation to a review board

I successfully identified 16 out of 24 flags, which I consider a strong result for my first CTF completed independently.

---

## Skills Demonstrated

- Incident response and threat hunting
- Log analysis using SIEM data
- Understanding of attack lifecycle (phishing → execution → persistence → lateral movement → C2)
- MITRE ATT&CK framework mapping
- Technical communication through executive reporting
- Cybersecurity presentation and briefing skills

---

## Key Takeaways

This CTF helped me understand how attackers operate in real environments and reinforced several important lessons:

- Phishing remains one of the most effective attack vectors
- Lateral movement is critical in full system compromise
- Domain controllers are high-value targets
- Detection and monitoring systems are essential for early response
- Cybersecurity requires both technical and communication skills
