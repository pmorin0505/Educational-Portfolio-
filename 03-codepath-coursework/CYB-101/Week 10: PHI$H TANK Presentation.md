## Project Topic

### Ransomware Attacks

Our presentation focused on ransomware as a major cybersecurity threat affecting modern organizations.

Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts files and demands payment (ransom) in exchange for restoring access.

---

## Key Topics Covered in Presentation

### What is Ransomware?
- Malware that encrypts files
- Attackers demand payment to restore access
- Can completely shut down business operations

---

### Why This Matters
- Companies lose millions in recovery and downtime
- Critical systems can be completely shut down
- Sensitive data may be stolen or leaked

---

### Real-World Impact
- Examples such as **LockBit**
- Global targeting of businesses of all sizes
- “Double extortion” tactics (data encryption + data theft)
- Millions in financial damages per incident

---

### Attack Process
- Initial access through phishing or malicious files
- User interaction triggers infection
- Malware installs and disables security tools
- Lateral movement across networks
- Files encrypted
- Ransom demand issued
- Business operations disrupted

---

### Business Vulnerabilities
- Weak or reused passwords
- Lack of employee security training
- No data backup systems
- Outdated or unpatched software

---

## Mitigation Strategies

### 1. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Adds extra login verification layer
- Protects against stolen passwords
- Reduces phishing success rates
- Easy to implement across systems

---

### 2. Secure Backups
- Regular backups of critical data
- Stored offline or in cloud environments
- Enables full recovery without paying ransom
- Reduces downtime significantly

---

### 3. Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR)
- Monitors systems for suspicious activity
- Detects malware behavior in real time
- Can isolate infected machines automatically
- Helps stop ransomware before it spreads

---

## Final Recommendation

Our team recommended a combined security strategy:

- Implement MFA across all systems
- Maintain secure and regularly tested backups
- Deploy EDR for real-time threat detection

This layered approach provides both prevention and recovery capabilities.

---

## Business Value

- Reduces risk of millions in financial loss
- Minimizes operational downtime
- Improves overall security posture
- Protects customer trust and company reputation

---

## Conclusion

Ransomware is one of the most dangerous modern cybersecurity threats due to its ability to disrupt entire organizations.

However, with proper security controls such as MFA, backups, and EDR, the risk can be significantly reduced.

This capstone demonstrated how cybersecurity decisions must balance risk, cost, and operational impact to protect real-world businesses.
