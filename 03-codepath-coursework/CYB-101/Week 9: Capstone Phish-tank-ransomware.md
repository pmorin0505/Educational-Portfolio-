# Week 9: CYB101 Capstone Prep – PHI$H TANK

## Overview

This week was a preparation and planning week for the CYB101 Group Capstone Project: **PHI$H TANK**.

Instead of a technical lab, this week focused on selecting a cybersecurity risk topic and beginning to develop a structured presentation with a team.

The goal was to prepare for a mock “investment pitch” where we will present a cybersecurity risk and proposed mitigation strategies to a panel of “executives.”

---

## Project Focus

For this capstone project, we are expected to:

- Identify a cybersecurity risk (from course units or independent research)
- Analyze why the risk is significant
- Research and evaluate at least two mitigation strategies
- Compare mitigation strategies based on cost, effectiveness, and practicality
- Provide a final recommendation to executives

---

## Work Completed This Week

During Week 9, the team focused on:

- Forming groups (3–8 students)
- Reviewing possible cybersecurity risks from previous units
- Selecting a topic for the final presentation
- Beginning initial research on risks and mitigations
- Outlining the structure of the presentation slides
- Dividing responsibilities among team members

---

## Chosen Topic

For our capstone presentation, our team selected:

**Ransomware Attacks**

We chose this topic because ransomware is a high-impact cybersecurity threat that affects individuals, businesses, and large organizations by encrypting data and demanding payment for recovery.

---

## Why This Matters

Ransomware is important to study because:

- It can cause major financial loss
- It can shut down critical systems and services
- It often spreads through phishing or weak security practices
- Recovery can be expensive or impossible without backups

---

## Next Steps

In the upcoming weeks, we will:

- Complete research on ransomware attack methods
- Identify real-world examples and case studies
- Develop mitigation strategies (backup systems, endpoint protection, user training, etc.)
- Build presentation slides for PHI$H TANK
- Practice presenting as a team for Week 10
