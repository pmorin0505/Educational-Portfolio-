## Ethical Dilemmas in Cybersecurity Whistleblowing

In today's digital era, cybersecurity and IT professionals, along with government insiders, often face ethical challenges when they uncover vulnerabilities that threaten public safety. While laws strictly regulate access to sensitive information, they do not always ensure institutions act in ways that protect the public from harm.

Recent whistleblower complaints involving Social Security data breaches highlight the tension between legal restrictions and moral responsibility. When official reporting channels fail, whistleblowers may expose millions of individuals to identity theft risks but also bring critical issues to light.

This raises an important question: should individuals be punished for breaking the law if their actions prevent greater harm?

This essay explores whistleblowing through ethical frameworks, arguing that in some cases, whistleblowing may be morally justified even when it violates legal boundaries.
